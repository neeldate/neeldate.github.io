Some more beliefs

I want to choose my environment.
Sometimes, that environment will be conducive to excellence;
sometimes, it won't.
That's okay.
Unhosen excellence can lead to unhappiness.

Deliberate discipline is worth its fickleness.
I want the freedom to do nothing yet choose to do something good.
I may have what feel like long phases of doing nothing.
But "it's the silence between the notes that makes the music."
Moreover, when faced with a major change that makes unproductivity non-optional,
I'll still be happy.

"Always tell the truth" has an important caveat.
Each human has feedback that they respond better to.
There are general principles that are valid for most of us.
Most of us, including me, hold a core belief that productivity and grit are important.
That is our truth.
But when are stuck in a rut, most of us need to be told it is okay even if we do nothing.
Strictly speaking, we need to be lied to.
But this lie captures a fundamental truth, that we respond better to some false messages.

I am skeptical of categorising myself for the sake of simplicity of identity.
That serves as a pigeonhole for future beliefs.
It is possible to be equally passionate about ideas from two opposing schools of thought.
Are you liberal or conservative? Open or private? Talkative or quiet?
My answer is: yes. All that, and more.
I am what I am, and there does not need to be a single word to encapsulate that complexity.
That's one reason we have names.

Measure productivity on the scale of life.
Play the long game.
Go slow to go far.
So slow that you often feel lazy.
Let joy be your guide for effort.

I dislike advice of the form
"most people are such and such; don't be like most people; be better than them."
Don't promote a divide between doers and non-doers of an activity.
That fosters a false and toxic feeling of superiority.
You are not better than anyone.
Everyone has a story.
