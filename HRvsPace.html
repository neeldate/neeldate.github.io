Progressive HR Vs. Pace Test

This test is useful to:

  • track progress over time.
  • learn how various intensities/zones feel.

How To Do It

Note: warmup jog is optional as it is built into the test.
  • Pick a route whose start and finish points are the same, is 1km to 2km long, and not hilly.
  • Run the above route around 5bpm above the lowest heart rate at which you can run at all. Embarrassingly easy. Measure how long it takes.
  • Once finished, run the route again at a heart rate 10bpm more than last time.
  • Keep moving up in steps of 10bpm after each completion of the route.
  • Do this for 30 to 80 minutes (4 to 8 steps) depending on how this run fits into your weekly plan. Very fit athletes can go for longer and still get valid results.

Tips

  • Use the target heart rates as caps. It is hard to stay at a precise heart rate, so try to stay within 5bpm less than the target heart rate.
  • If you exceed the target heart rate, back off.
  • Use the test to compare to your own past tests, not to others. Heart rate monitors are not standardised and accurate enough yet.
  • Increasing heart rate by 10bpm takes a minute or two. This does not matter because of the previous point.
  • If you really want accurate heart rate-wise paces, use steps of 5bpm instead. The transition time is much shorter.
