Easy Training For The Time-Crunched Athlete

Disclaimer: I have not experienced being a time-crunched athlete. However, I have thought often about how I would approach my training if I had a busy work and family life, maybe planning for a possible future.

Committing 2 hours at a time may be impossible, especially on weekdays. What are possible ways to combat this?

  • Can I manage 2 x 1-hour windows?
  • What about 1 hour in the morning, 30min in the afternoon, and 30min in the evening?
  • Can I commit more hours on the weekend?

If you are not doing your sessions at a super easy intensity, the answer to both is NO.

The Case For Only Going Easy

You have less time but still want to improve.

  • "I have less time, so I will use it 'better' by going faster."

This is our default setting. But without enough easy volume, improvement will stop after some time.

Also, in our mind, "training" = "hard". Motivation is finite. If we burn out, we lose the progress we have made.

Instead, I suggest:

  • Always go easy.
  • See if you can get your frequency up by reducing duration.
  • Easy will make it fun. Maybe once it is fun, you will come up with a crazy idea to make more time.

  • Easy activity requires almost no warmup. There's some extra time.
  • Easy activity shortens recovery. Maybe you'll be fresher at work and get it done earlier.
  • Easy activity is reduces binge-eating. Anyone who has struggled with that knows that those episodes steal time.

  • You won't be this busy forever.
  • When you become less busy, don't you want a strong aerobic base?
  • You can build that right now, by going easy.

But Won't My Race Performance Suffer?

For races 5km or longer, probably not. And if it does, then by how much? Seconds? At the recreational level, does it matter?

What is the cost of those few seconds? Do I still want to be training 2 years from now?
