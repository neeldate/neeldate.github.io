My Training And Coaching Philosophy

Want to see this philosophy in action? My 2023 plan.

  • High Level Ideas

    • Enjoy endurance training for life.
    • Go fast, go long at 50, 70, 90 years old.

    • Comfort being unstimulated and alone.
    • Yes, this almost always means NO music. Ditch the headphones.

    • Work in Olympic cycles. Don't worry about shorter timescales.
    • Choose one distance class to focus on for those 4 years. Don't worry: there will still be plenty of variety.
    • There is scope for racing other distances too, just that a full block will not be dedicated to them (see very last point under "Year Structure" below).

    • 2 years for base-building (some fast stuff included), 1 year for performance, 1 year for health and maintenance.
    • Eliminate self-sabotaging habits that cause unplanned misses (eg. the extra-extra piece of cake last night).

  • Year Structure

    • Train for 48 weeks out of 52.
    • Every 4th week recovery (50% to 80% of usual volume).

    • Divide year into aerobic, threshold, and specific seasons. Borrowed from Nils van der Poel. That is a gem of a read.
    • Aerobic season lasts 60% to 80% of the year, the other two 10% to 20% each.

    • Those who MUST have non-easy sessions year-round: short threshold sessions or short races can be done up to once a week, year round. But they come at the expense of aerobic work. Personally, I don't do them. I prefer long easy movement anyway.

  • Aerobic Season: The Most Important

    • Maximise volume by keeping intensity as low as it takes (see "Volume" section towards the end of article).

    • We build capacity to be able to do more intense training later (at the end of the year).
    • More aerobic capacity => better recovery between hard sessions => ability to do more hard sessions.

    • Best ways to keep intensity low are easy cycling and walking.
    • The limiter to doing more volume, for almost all of us, is psychological, not physical.
    • We can always add walking or easy cycling volume without risking injury or illness.

    • To do long hours, it is important to stay motivated and keep things fun.
    • Ask yourself: what would this look like if it were fun?
    • In the middle of aerobic season, we will do an endurance challenge (eg. a marathon or ultramarathon). Fun motivator to ramp up easy volume.

  • Week Structure

    • Train 5 days in a row, rest 2 consecutive days.
    • "Rest" can be anything from sleeping all day to hiking all day with friends as a social activity. Just have fun and don't generate fatigue that will impact next week's training.

    • One workout per week to work on focus point of current season of the year (see "Year Structure" above).
    • Sometimes a second workout as a taste for next season of the year, or when you feel strong and have been getting enough rest.

    • Focus of workouts to run smooth, not to feel pain.
    • Once we know we can go fast, we become more comfortable going slow.

  • Frequency

    • 10 or more endurance training activities per week (including walking), plus basic strength and stability work.
    • Try to fit the above 10 sessions into 5 days. At least 2 activities per day, 1 of which is a 30+ minute walk.
    • If you have time but cannot tolerate this week after week, your training is too intense. Slow down.

  • Volume

    • Measured in units of time, with distance targets for each week tailored to specific athlete.

    • For most, this is the biggest limiter.
    • Nothing better for your aerobic capacity than volume.
    • Nothing better for racing fast than aerobic capacity. So,

    • In the initial YEARS, just focus on increasing volume.
    • Yes, this means devote more time.
    • If you don't seem to have more time, consider this.

    • Increasing volume by running more is slow and risky.
    • Cycling is the best way to increase volume. Nearly everyone can tolerate an easy hour a day on the bike without reducing other activities.
    • If you hate cycling, give it one last shot. Go super slow. If you still hate it, walking is the next best activity for risk-free volume.

  • Intensity

    • If you haven't, read the article on Zones.
    • No more than 10% of total time each week spent in zones other than easy/steady (Zone 3/4/5, yellow/red zones, heavy/severe domains).
    • No more than 5% of total time each week spent in Zone 5/red zone/severe domain.
    • The above figures are maximums. For me in aerobic season, maybe 1% of total time is not easy/steady.

    • Intensity can never substitute for volume. The purpose is completely different.
    • Intensity is earned.

    • If we don't feel like going on a run, we tend to make it short and fast. Or we do a home workout that gets the blood flowing and makes us feel productive or sexy.
    • Don't do that. You are jeopardising your training. Go for a walk instead. Or walk at home. Or sleep. Whatever you do, keep the intensity LOW.

The above philosophy in action: my 2023 plan.
