Neel Date

Happiness is my duty but not a universal duty

I am lucky.
My life has been fair, perhaps even unfairly forgiving.
Thank you, Nature.
This may not be true for everyone, but my happiness is my choice.
And choosing to be happy is my virtue.

I must not judge others by my notions of virtue and vice.
The only person you can be better than is the version of yourself that didn't choose the right option.
Virtue exists. There is a right way to live.
But only personally, not globally.

Which compels me to restate: happiness is a virtuous choice for me.
Seeking happiness is not a universal duty.
This took years to understand, then accept.
I believe one's access to happiness is determined by the amount of free will that one has.
Since not everyone has equal free will, not everyone has equal access to happiness.
However, anyone can be happy.
Who am I to tell someone their disadvantages impose an upper bound to their happiness?
Empower the disadvantaged to overturn their reality without burdening them to do so.
