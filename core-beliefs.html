Some core beliefs

Beliefs need not be true.
Beliefs may change at any time.

I am lucky.
My life has been fair, perhaps even unfairly forgiving.
Thank you, Nature.
This may not be true for everyone, but my happiness is my choice.
And choosing to be happy is my virtue.

The negative inner voice is a beautiful part of the human experience.
Acknowledge it, smile, then transcend it.
Fear is an indicator that you are walking a worthwhile path.
Do it despite the fear.

Productivity and grit are important.
But this is not the message we need to hear when we have lost them.
You can't summon the will to get stuff done through sheer force.
At least not without significant side effects.
A way to reintegrate productivity and grit into your identity:
convince yourself that they are not that important.
Give yourself the permission to half ass.
Find a way to get started.
Once started, your proclivity for consistent hard work will win.

Just action is not enough.
One needs action and intention.
That is how you access the full package of benefits of the action.
Do it for the right reasons.
Getting your intentions in order will stall action temporarily, but that's the point.
I'd rather move slowly to my destination than rapidly in the opposite direction.

If you want joy, go get it.
Chasing joy is a useful approach to being happy;
the Greek philosophy of Epicureanism got it right.
Paradoxically, voluntary discomfort is the most reliable way to craft joy.
Stoicism and Epicureanism advocate for similar lifestyles to different audiences.
I resonate with both.

Free will is central to my value system.
Simply following your whims will lead you along a virtuous path.
Humans have a predisposition for good.
Have faith that what you want will evolve into what you want to want.
Some day, the noblest path will also be the path of least resistance.
