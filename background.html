Neel Date

Sporting Background

2008 (age 6)

Able to cycle 20km.
Swam twice a week.
Mix of play and stamina building with my father on many weekends.

2010

Able to cycle 50km in 3:30hrs.
Swam once a week.
Still out with my father on most weekends with good weather.
Started playing cricket.
Able to be active all day in hot and humid weather.
Loved competing and trying hard.

2012

Climbed Mt. Fuji for first time.
Still swimming once a week. Could swim 500m without stopping.

2013

First time distance running (2km race in 9:52).
I could get into a rhythm and then running felt tolerable, even fun.

2014

Stopped swimming.
Climbed Mt Fuji in cold, wind, and rain. Test of character.
Ran every few months. No structure. Faster each time than the last.
Enjoyed pushing myself and giving everything I had.

2016

Cricket on most weekends.
Able to cycle 130km in around 8 hours.
Able to run 21km.
Became more self-motivated to train.

2017

Focused on cricket and began training in the offseason.

2018

Paid more attention to supplementary things such as diet, strength, speed, and stamina.
Ambition was to set the standard of skill, fitness, attitude, and professionalism for younger players.
Ran every few weeks, mostly for challenging myself and learning to move fast while in some pain.

2019

Training for U19 World Cup. Unsustainably intense but with a clear and important objective.
Only two intensities in everything I did: hard and very hard.
Pushing body close to its limit.

Jan 2020 (age 18)

U19 World Cup. Peak of my cricket. Rewarding experience. Emotionally challenging but fulfilling.
After the tournament was over, my initial emotion was fear that the peak of my cricket had come and gone.
I was even more afraid of how hard I would need to push myself to ensure that my best still lay ahead.
Even so, I intended to put in the work over the next few years to take my cricket to the next level.
But Covid-19 was just around the corner, and things were about to change.
Over the next two years, I would become a runner. Cricket would exit my identity.
